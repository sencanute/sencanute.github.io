Project Presentation

Reimagining Japantown is an augmented reality (AR) remapping of Vancouver’s historical Japantown, developed in Unity for Android devices. As a descendent of Japanese Canadian internees myself, this project was both a push to challenge my creative boundaries and a personal exploration of time, space, place, and cultural memory.

As users physically navigate Powell Street, corresponding archival materials appear as they approach GPS coordinates of designated historical sites. Archival material is overlaid onto a live camera view of the user’s surroundings, merging past and present onto one screen. A non-AR implementation of the project allows users to bookmark sites of interest and access archival images and site descriptions remotely. At each site, cultural information is embedded into first person encounters with the current landscape. Going beyond passive viewings of material, users must actively engage with the past while situating themselves within the context of the present
